Melbourne Exhibition and Convention Centre



The Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre is a Meetings and Conference Centre located in Melbourne CBD. The Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre were voted as Australasia’s No. 1 in Meetings Conference Centre in 2014, 2013 and 2012 by the respected World Travel Awards.



The Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre is owned by the Victorian State Government and functioned by the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Trust. The Architecture of the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre has won prestigious awards and is absolutely majestic.



The Exhibition Centre released in 1996 and was shaped by Denton Corker Marshall. The main characteristic of the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre is the blade entrance that is slanting at a position and sustained by a couple of yellow rods. This makes the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre one of the Melbourne’s most iconic buildings. In 1996 the Melbourne Convention and Exhibition Centre was bestowed the Sir Zelman Cowen Award.
