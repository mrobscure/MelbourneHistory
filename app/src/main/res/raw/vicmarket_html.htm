Queen Vic Market



The Queen Victoria market is also referred as ‘Vic Market’ or ‘Queen Vic’, and has been residing in the Melbourne CBD for more than a century. Queen Victoria Market is a remarkable milestone of Melbourne that is almost ranges approximately two city blocks. Queen Victoria Market is a lively and busy inner-city Market where people can shop for almost entirely from Australian fruits and vegetables, and local and imported variety of foods, to essentials, fashion and relics.



Queen Victoria Market is 130 years, and before it came to being the Queen Victoria Market it has been a cemetery, a livestock market and finally a wholesale fruit and vegetable market. The Queen Victoria Market was formally released on 20 March 1878, and it is the major and most integral of all Melbourne’s pronounced 19th century bazaars.



Queen Victoria Market Spans over 7 hectares of land within Melbourne CBD. When visiting the Queen Victoria Market make sure to stroll through the ‘Heritage Trail’ to discover the historical significance of the Queen Victoria market via its exceptional sculptures, rooftops and facades. You will be amazed to discover the buildings, events and people who assisted the construction of the Queen Victoria Market. You will be surprised to know that majority of the buildings are still used in trade today.



In 1842 The Melbourne City Council was designed to look afterwards the City’s multiple markets, among them one would go one to become the Queen Victoria Market. In March 1859 the Melbourne Town Hall approved the Meat Market allocation to be recycled as a market. Finally on 20 March 1878 The Queen Victoria Market officially opened to the public. Then in 1917 Melbourne General Market Lands Act was implemented that facilitated the growth of Queen Victoria Market on Old Cemetery land. On 1st December 1969 Wholesale Fruit and Vegetable Market repositioned from Queen Victoria Market to Footscray. Queen Victoria Sunday Market trade off began on 25 March 1979. Cooking school opened and Night Market began dealings on 25 November 1998. In 2000 the complete Queen Victoria Market and Old Melbourne Cemetery were categorized by the National Trust.
