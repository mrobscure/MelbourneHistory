Eureka Tower



Eureka Tower is the tallest building in the southern hemisphere and is 14th tallest residential building in the world. About 297.3-metre in height, the Eureka skyscraper situated in the Southbank precinct of Melbourne, Victoria, Australia. Eureka Tower construction initially begain in August 2002 was formally opened on 11 October 2006.



The Eureka Tower project was planned by Melbourne architectural firm Fender Katsalidis Architects and was built by Grocon. Eureka Tower is currently the 108th tallest building in the world. The observation deck of Eureka Tower also known as Eureka Skydeck 88 inhabits the complete 88th floor of the Eureka Tower and is the highest public vantage point in a building in the Southern Hemisphere and was opened to the public on 15 May 2007.



Eureka Tower has got a Observation Deck, Residence Tower and the Melbourne CBD's most impressive and exciting dining place Eureka 89.
