Melbourne Museum



Melbourne Museum discovers life in Victoria, from the natural environment that is mapped to our culture and history. Melbourne Museum is positioned in the beautiful Carlton Gardens, opposite the award winning historic Royal Exhibition Building that is another landmark of Melbourne, housing an enduring collection of eight galleries, with one just dedicated for children.



The Melbourne Museum attractions comprise of a complete skeleton of a blue whale, the Bunjilaka Aboriginal Cultural Centre, a living rainforest, the racehorse Phar Lap and an IMAX theatre on site. Furthermore, the Melbourne museum hosts and displays a number of international exhibitions in the touring hall for example mummies from Egypt and dinosaurs from China.



The Melbourne Museum was planned by Denton Corker Marshall Architects and completed structure in 2001.The Melbourne museum is the largest museum in the Southern Hemisphere, and is another site of Museum Victoria, which also controls the Immigration Museum and Science works Museum.



The Melbourne Museum has seven key gallerias, a Children's Gallery and a provisional exhibit gallery on three levels, Upper, Ground and Lower Level and was fashioned by Baulderstone Hornibrook.



In addition, the museum has other facilities such as the Sidney Myer Amphitheatre and The Age Theatre. The Discovery Centre, on the Lower Level, is a free public research centre. The museum also has a cafe and a souvenir shop. The IMAX Theatre is also part of the Melbourne Museum showing mainly movies, usually documentary films, in 3-D format.
