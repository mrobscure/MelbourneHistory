Royal Botanical Gardens



The Royal Botanic Gardens Melbourne was set up in 1846 by Lieutenant Governor Charles La Trobe and is situated near the city centre of Melbourne, on the banks (south side) of the Yarra River. Royal Botanical Gardens is one of the major landmarks of the Melbourne CBD.



Since its establishment in 1846 and the next consecutive 60 years, this damp site was converted into the world-famed backdrop and globally popular botanical gardens referred as Melbourne’s Botanic Gardens. In 1958, Queen Elizabeth II conferred the ‘Royal’ preface on the Botanic Gardens. In the present day, the Royal Botanic Gardens is vastly extended to the former established in 1846 on its inner-city Melbourne location.



Royal Melbourne Botanical Gardens integrates an amount of different places and functions. The Royal Botanic Gardens Melbourne are next to a greater collection of parks, in the middle of St. Kilda Road and the Yarra River referred to as the Domain Parklands, that comprises of Kings Domain, Alexandra Gardens and Queen Victoria Gardens.

The Royal Botanical Gardens are internationally renowed gardens with many attractions built in.The Ian Potter Foundation Children’s Garden attracts a lot of visitors and is one of the utmost prevalent features in the gardens where kids can enjoy playing in the ruin garden, meeting place, wetland area, bamboo forest, gorge, plant tunnel, rill and kitchen garden. Some of the tourist attractions of the Royal Botanic Gardens include the National Herbarium, Tropical Hothouse, Plant Craft Cottage and the Guilfoyle’s Volcano water reservoir. The Royal Botanic Gardens provides Visitor services and amenities that comprise of a visitor centre, guided walks, interpretive signs, two cafes and the Gardens Shop to all its visitors
