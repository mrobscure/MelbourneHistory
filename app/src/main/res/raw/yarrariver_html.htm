Yarra River



Yarra River is a key source of water and transport for the Melbournians. Melbourne was built on the banks of the Yarra River in 1835.



The Yarra River starts from the Yarra Ranges and streams towards the West for further than 240 km (148 miles) through country and outer Melbourne to the city centre and Docklands, where it discharges into Port Phillip Bay. In the present day Melbourne, the Yarra River passes through the Yarra Promenade and Flinders Street Station in the heart of Melbourne.



On the banks of the Yarra River in Melbourne Sight, it is a common sight to see Rowers stroke past from the neighbouring Royal Botanic Gardens, and boats cruise up and down the Yarra.



Some of the most scintillating views of Melbourne can be witnessed while cruising along the Yarra. One of the hot spots of Melbourne CBD.
