Old Treasury Building



The Old Treasury Building is one of the landmarks of the Melbourne CBD and used to home of the Treasury Department of the Government of Victoria but has now been set up as museum.The Old Treasury Building was developed between1858–62 and was an unofficial capital building of Australia at one time.



In the modern day world, the Old Treasury Building depicts a detailed description of Victoria's history from the 1830s, underlined by key documents and artefacts from Public Record Office Victoria (PROV).



These documents and artefacts once located in the Old Treasury Building gold vaults discover themes of Indigenous Victorians and first white settlement in 1835, Ned Kelly and Criminals, Victorian Democracy, Victorians at Work and the Gold Rush.
