Shrine of Remembrance



The Shrine of Remembrance, Melbourne is of historic, communal, architectural and artistic importance to the State of Victoria. The Shrine of Remembrance is another major landmark of Melbourne that was formed to as a shrine for the extensive loss of lives in the First World War (1914 â€“18).



A total of 114,000 Victorians were recruited in the First World War out of which 89,000 aided overseas. Among the 89000 soldiers, a total of 19,000 soldiers were killed. These soldiers were laid to rest in graves far from home during a period in which many Australians did not travel overseas.



The Shrine acts a resting place for all these soldiers and their respective families where they can mourn honour and reserve the memories of these fallen soldiers.The Shrine was constructed to remember those who had aided in the First World War and also to honour their courage of the men, women and children who stayed at home.



The Shrine is established in a straight line with Swanston Street and is the most prevailing of the three long landscapes in Melbourne. The outlook is significantly heightened by the neighbouring open parks which reside the Shrine in segregation from other buildings and from the French avenue flair of the tree plantings on St. Kilda Road.
