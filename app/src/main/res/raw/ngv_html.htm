National Gallery of Victoria



The National Gallery of Victoria (NGV) is Australia’s ancient and renowned public art museum that hosts various exhibitions and compromises of some of the world’s finest art collections like treasures from ancient Egypt, Greece, Rome and the pre-Columbian period, in addition to an Oceanic gallery dedicated to the indigenous cultures of the Pacific area.



The National Gallery of Victoria also comprises of approximately 16,000 global designs and illustrations, an illustrious range of European and Asian ornamental arts, and a galleria space devoted to 4000 works signifying the art of Asia.



The National Gallery of Victoria showcases many of the world’s greatest art collections such as the Masterpieces from the Hermitage: The Legacy of Catherine the Great that include works of great artists such as Rembrandt, Rubens, Velazquez and Van Dyck. Through these artists the National Gallery of Victoria showcases the finest art work of Dutch and Flemish.



The National Gallery of Victoria also contains an exceptional collection of European and British paintings going back to 1200 to the present day.



Furthermore, the NGV celebrates the varied and exceptional water forms of Australia that includes flood plains, swamps, creeks and alpine lakes through the works of John Wolseley who has explored the wetlands of Australia over a period of 4 years. Undoubtedly the National Gallery of Victoria is another hot spot for anyone visiting Melbourne.
